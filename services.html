Deep Blue Documents Services

Overview

The University of Michigan (U-M) Library offers deposit, access, and preservation services to support Deep Blue Documents and its users. Additionally, the U-M Library provides community management, end-user support, and system management services to provide ongoing support for Deep Blue Documents communities, respond to user inquiries, and supply system monitoring, back-up, and recovery.

Deposit Services

Deep Blue Documents offers contributors a flexible, easy-to-use deposit process. A depositor completes a brief online deposit form and grants non-exclusive permission for Deep Blue Documents to distribute and preserve the Work. The deposit process includes the following:

  • Register with Deep Blue Documents and edit their user profile
  • Add a deposited Work to a specified collection
  • Enter required descriptive information (metadata) for a Work
  • Include additional optional metadata to describe a Work
  • Bundle multiple files of various formats in a single Work
  • Identify formats of deposited files
  • Grant a non-exclusive license to U-M to distribute a Work in accordance with the specified distribution policy and translate it for the purposes of preservation.

Access Services

Deep Blue Documents' Access Services give users the ability to browse, search, and discover Works of interest, request one or more Works from the system, and take delivery of the requested Works. Those interested in accessing the contents of Deep Blue Documents can:

  • Locate Works via online search engines (Google, Bing, etc.) and the U-M Library’s Articles Search
  • Browse Deep Blue Documents contents by title, author, collection or community
  • Search for Works anywhere in Deep Blue Documents, or within a particular community or collection
  • Search for matches within author, title, or full text words and view results
  • View a Work overview, including: Deep Blue Documents core metadata, list of collections that contain this Work, and links to each of the files (text, image, etc.) that comprise the Work
  • View the contents of a Work online
  • Download an individual Work and view it within a web browser or save the file to a specified location (if the Work doesn’t have any access restrictions).

Repository Services

Deep Blue Documents provides storage and preservation services to ensure the longevity of deposited Works. Users should be assured that Works deposited in many formats (see Preservation Policy) will be accessible even when the original application in which it was created is obsolete and the means to access it are inoperable. For each Work, Deep Blue Documents:

  • Provides persistent storage, including appropriate back-up and recovery procedures
  • Assigns a persistent identifier (a Digital Object Identifier, DOI) that will not change and is appropriate to cite in other Works
  • Supports a range of service levels for preservation and notifies users of Deep Blue Documents’ preservation constraints
  • Stores information about the Work’s origin and any updates, if applicable
  • Maintains an auditable history and record of changes to Deep Blue Documents

Collection Management Services

Deep Blue Documents’ Collection Management Services are a set of consultative and applied services designed to meet the needs of individual collections. U-M Library staff will provide guidance in developing a collection's workflow process, metadata requirements, and distribution policies as well as launching a Deep Blue Documents community homepage.

The U-M Library manages all aspects of the collection set-up process, including:

  • Planning and scheduling new collection set-ups
  • At the request of a user, facilitating an initial collection discussion and explaining set-up requirements
  • Assisting in training collection administrators
  • Implementing custom workflow for the depositing process including metadata defaults if needed
  • Assisting in customizing Deep Blue Documents collection home pages
  • Specifying a collection's distribution policy and license requirements
  • Coordinating batch uploads of historic content and metadata.

End-User Support Services

The U-M Library will provide support to all Deep Blue Documents users. Support will be provided Monday through Friday, 9:00am to 5:00pm Eastern time (excluding University holidays). Inquiries will typically receive a return response via email within five business days.

The Deep Blue Documents webpage provides documentation in the About and Help sections to answer routine questions and user support contact information for more complex questions.

System Management Services

System Management Services are the back-office support for Deep Blue Documents, working to ensure optimal system performance and high availability.

The U-M Library will provide a comprehensive set of technical services for Deep Blue Documents system operations, including:

  • Performing system monitoring, testing, and debugging
  • Performing system administration
  • Monitoring and upgrading Deep Blue Documents utility programs and middleware
  • Developing approved system enhancements
  • Performing bulk loading of historic content and metadata
  • Maintaining a registry of known content and metadata formats for preservation purposes.

The Deep Blue Documents Systems Administrator will provide System Management Services with back-up from Library Information Technology’s Digital Library Applications and Architecture and Engineering departments. The U-M Library provides continuing support for Deep Blue Documents’ server, storage, and network operations.
